LIBRA

Information

Libra (♎︎) (Ancient Greek: Ζυγός, romanized: Zygós, Latin for "scales") is the seventh astrological sign in the zodiac. It spans 180°–210° celestial longitude. The Sun transits this sign on average between September 22 and October 23. The symbol of the scales is based on the Scales of Justice held by Themis, the Greek personification of divine law and custom. She became the inspiration for modern depictions of Lady Justice. The ruling planet of Libra is Venus. Libra is the only zodiac sign that is represented by an object; with the other eleven signs represented by either an animal or mythological character.

How it formed

The Libra constellation is one of the 12 zodiac constellations and is located in the southern sky. It is believed to have originated from ancient Babylonian and Greek cultures as a representation of the scales of justice. The stars in the Libra constellation were likely named and mapped by astronomers in the Babylonian era. The constellation was later adopted by Greeks, who associated it with the goddess Themis, the deity of divine law and order. According to ancient Greek mythology, the Libra constellation represents the scales of justice held by Astraea, the goddess of justice. She was known to weigh the souls of the dead before deciding their fate.

Where and when to find Libra

The Libra constellation can be best spotted in the night sky during the late spring and summer months, specifically between April and July in the Northern Hemisphere, and between October and December in the Southern Hemisphere. To locate Libra, find the star Spica in the large constellation Virgo. From there, move southeast to locate a relatively dim, kite-shaped group of stars, which form Libra. It will be approximately halfway between the bright star Antares (in the constellation Scorpius) and Spica. Keep in mind that the visibility of the constellation depends on your exact location, local weather conditions, and light pollution.
