PISCES

Information

Pisces (♓︎) (/ˈpaɪsiːz/; Ancient Greek: Ἰχθύες Ikhthyes, Latin for "fishes") is the twelfth and final astrological sign in the zodiac. It is a negative, mutable sign. It spans 330° to 360° of celestial longitude. Under the tropical zodiac, the sun transits this area between February 19 and March 20.[a] In classical interpretations, the symbol of the fish is derived from the ichthyocentaurs, who aided Aphrodite when she was born from the sea. According to some tropical astrologers, the current astrological age is the Age of Pisces, while others maintain that currently it is the Age of Aquarius.

How it formed

The formation of the Pisces constellation dates back to ancient times. The Greeks and Romans both had stories about these stars, which they named after their word for fish. One of the most famous myths surrounding Pisces is that of Aphrodite and Eros fleeing a monster by turning into fish and jumping into a river. The two then swam away to safety, and the constellation of Pisces was said to commemorate their miraculous escape. Over time, many different cultures have interpreted the meaning of these stars in various ways. In astrology, people born under the sign of Pisces are said to be imaginative, intuitive, and empathetic.

Where and when to find Pisces

Pisces can be found in the night sky during the months of February and March. The best time to observe Pisces is during mid-March, when it is highest in the sky around midnight. To find Pisces, locate the constellation Pegasus and look for the two fish swimming away from each other, connected by a thin line. They are typically located to the west of Pegasus. It may be easiest to identify Pisces using a star chart or a mobile app that can identify constellations.

Fun fact !

Pisces is the twelfth and final sign of the zodiac and is represented by two fish swimming in opposite directions, which signifies the duality of Pisces' nature. Those born under this sign are known for their emotional depth, sensitivity, and creative talents. Pisceans are also believed to be intuitive, understanding, and compassionate individuals who possess a unique ability to tap into both their own and others' emotions.
