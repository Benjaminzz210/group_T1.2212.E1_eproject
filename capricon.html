CAPRICON

Information

Capricorn (♑︎) (Greek: Αιγόκερως, romanized: Aigókerōs, Latin for "horned goat") is the tenth astrological sign in the zodiac out of twelve total zodiac signs, originating from the constellation of Capricornus, the goat.[2] It spans the 270–300th degree of the zodiac, corresponding to celestial longitude. Under the tropical zodiac, the sun transits this area from about December 22 to January 19. In astrology, Capricorn is considered an earth sign, negative sign, and one of the four cardinal signs. Capricorn is said to be ruled by the planet Saturn. There appears to be a connection between traditional characterizations of Capricorn as a sea goat and the Sumerian god of wisdom and waters,[5] who also had the head and upper body of a goat and the lower body and tail of a fish.[6] Later known as Ea in Akkadian and Babylonian mythology, Enki was the god of intelligence (gestú, literally "ear"), creation, crafts; magic; water, seawater and lake water.

How it formed

According to Greek mythology, the constellation Capricorn was formed when the god Pan transformed into a fish in order to escape the monster Typhon. As he was crossing a river, he saw the goddess Aphrodite and her son Eros, who were in danger of drowning. Pan helped them to safety and as a reward, Aphrodite immortalized him in the heavens as the constellation Capricorn. In other cultures, Capricorn was associated with various animals such as the goat, deer, or antelope. Regardless of the mythology or culture, Capricorn has always been a symbol of strength, endurance, and ambition. Capricorns are known for their practicality, hard work, and determination - all qualities that are associated with the goat, the animal most commonly associated with the Capricorn zodiac sign.

Where and when to find Capricorn

Capricorn is a zodiac constellation that can be found in the winter sky in the Northern Hemisphere and the summer sky in the Southern Hemisphere. It is located between the constellations Aquarius and Sagittarius. The best time to see Capricorn is during the months of December and January for those in the Northern Hemisphere, and June and July for those in the Southern Hemisphere. It can be seen in the southeastern sky around midnight.

Fun fact !

Capricorn is the 10th astrological sign in the Zodiac and is represented by the symbol of the sea-goat, which combines the lower body of a fish with the upper body of a goat. This sign is associated with ambition, discipline, responsibility, and practicality, and those born under this sign are said to be hardworking and determined individuals who strive for success in their careers and personal lives. Famous Capricorns include Martin Luther King Jr., Muhammad Ali, Kate Middleton, and Michelle Obama.
